# "Two Worlds" — Solo Recording Guide + Script

## What this is
A follow-along shooting plan for **one person filming themselves** (Tim), no crew. Phone or camera on a tripod, one lamp, your own desk + kitchen. Goal: the CH02 "Two Worlds" film — cross-cutting the cold **Operator** world against the warm **Father** world, same hands doing both, collapsing into one still frame, ending on a confession to camera.

Two things make this work with zero crew:
1. **Most shots are hands/objects on a tripod** — no one needs to hold the camera or be on screen.
2. **Your face is meant to be hidden anyway** — so "I'm filming myself" is a feature, not a problem.

---

## Gear (all minimal)
- Phone or camera on a **tripod** (a stack of books works). Lock exposure/focus manually so it doesn't hunt.
- **One lamp** you can move — a desk lamp or a warm bulb. That's your whole lighting kit.
- A dark room / corner you can control light in. Curtains closed, overheads off.
- **Record audio separately** for the spoken lines — phone voice-memo held close to your mouth, or earbuds mic. Clean voice matters more than clean picture.
- Optional: a strip of warm (orange) and cool (blue) gel, or just use a warm bulb for Father and daylight/screen glow for Operator.

---

## The two looks (how to flip between worlds with one lamp)

**OPERATOR (cold, wide, restless):**
- Light source = a **screen/monitor glow** or cool daylight from a window. Bluish.
- Frame **wide** — you small in a big dark room. Camera slightly handheld/drifting (or nudge the tripod).
- Curtains cracked for cold light, or point a laptop screen at yourself.

**FATHER (warm, close, still):**
- Light source = **one warm lamp at 45°** from the side, low. Everything else dark.
- Frame **close** — hands, side of face, back of neck. Camera dead still on the tripod.
- The center of your face stays in shadow. Turn so the lamp rims your edge.

You shoot the whole thing twice-lit: cool setup for Operator shots, warm setup for Father shots. Do all the cool ones, then re-light warm and do all the warm ones.

---

## The script (record these as voiceover — audio only)

Record your voice reading these, close to the mic, quiet and unhurried. Short declaratives. No performance — say it like it's true and you're a little tired of saying it. Leave silence between lines. Pulled from your interview transcript, in film order:

**Cold open / disqualifier**
> "If a man's not a fit, I tell him. On the first call. The man who'll tell you 'this isn't for you' before you've paid him is the only man worth paying."

**The Operator**
> "Pressure I earned instead of read about. You can study leadership for a decade and never once have a hundred and fifty people waiting on a call you don't want to make."
> "Fifty-one million dollars. Accountable for — not advising on. Accountable for."
> "We cut the failure rate by two-thirds."
> "People hear 'four-x capacity' and think growth. I think about the nights it didn't break."
> **"That clarity is a discipline, not a mood."** ← pull-quote 1
> "You stop getting to be confused in public. That's the trade."

**The Father (the hinge — slow down here)**
> "My son is neurodivergent."
> "There is no framework for that. I've built frameworks my whole career — for risk, for teams, for money — and the most important thing in my life doesn't respond to any of them."
> **"You don't manage it. You show up for it. Every day. Without a deck."** ← pull-quote 2
> "The man who told you balance is achievable has not met a five a.m. that belonged to nobody but the people who need him."

**The Convergence / confession (look at the lens)**
> "There's a specific man nobody is actually talking to. He's competent. From the outside he's winning. And he's alone. I built this for him. I was him."
> "A consigliere isn't your friend. He's more useful than that. He sees clearly and he says the thing."
> **"Not warmth. Accuracy."** ← pull-quote 3
> **"A straight answer. That's rarer than you'd think."** ← pull-quote 4 (then silence)

Record 2–3 takes of each line. The last two lines: record them very quiet, almost to yourself.

## Shot-by-shot record list

Shoot in this order (it's the efficient order, not the edit order). Each shot: set the tripod, lock focus/exposure, hit record, do the action slowly 2–3 times, stop. Keep every clip 8–15 seconds so you have room to cut.

### Block 1 — COOL setup (Operator). Cold light, wide-ish, camera can drift slightly.
1. **You, small in the dark room** — sit/stand at the desk, window or screen glow behind you, you as a silhouette. Just sit still, breathe. 15s.
2. **Hands: sign a document / initial a page.** Close, cold light, on the desk. 10s.
3. **Hands: knot a necktie.** 10s.
4. **Hands: set a phone face-down on the desk.** 8s.
5. **Pour coffee**, steam in the cool light. 10s.
6. **Empty desk / straighten a chair**, you leaving frame. 10s.
7. **Back of your neck / over the shoulder** at the desk, screen glow. 12s.

### Block 2 — Re-light WARM (Father). One lamp at 45°, everything close and still.
8. **You at the kitchen table, pre-dawn feel**, rim-lit, face dark. Just sit, unmoving — this is the longest hold. 20s.
9. **Hands: tie a child's shoe** (a small shoe on the floor works even solo). 10s.
10. **Hands: write a name on a lunch bag** or a school form. 10s.
11. **Pour cereal** into a bowl. 10s.
12. **Hands: set the same phone face-down** — this time on the kitchen table. 8s.
13. **Hand easing a door shut** (a bedroom door, slow). 10s.
14. **Push in a small chair** at the kitchen table. 8s.
15. *(Optional, only with consent)* a small hand or a bowl entering frame — child never identifiable. 8s.

### Block 3 — CONFESSION (warm, closest, camera dead still).
16. **Closest framing of you**, lamp to the side, face mostly dark. Turn slowly *toward* the lamp so it catches your edge. Two versions: (a) stay turned away, (b) turn until the light almost reveals you. 15s each.
17. **Final shot:** look toward the lens for one held beat, then reach over and dim/turn off the lamp so it goes to black. 15s.

**The matched pairs to make sure you got** (these are what you hard-cut between in the edit): tie ⟷ child's shoe · sign ⟷ lunch bag · phone-down (desk) ⟷ phone-down (table) · coffee ⟷ cereal · straighten chair ⟷ push in small chair.

## Audio you also want (record separately)
- **Operator room tone:** 30s of the office quiet + a faint electrical/keyboard hum.
- **Father room tone:** 30s of the kitchen — fridge hum, early-morning quiet.
- **The son, once:** a small off-screen sound (a hum, a spoon on a bowl) — *only if you have consent*; otherwise leave it as pure silence.
- 30s of **pure silence/room tone** for the ending.

---

## How to assemble it (edit order)
1. Lay the **voiceover** down first — it's the spine. Space the lines out; let silence sit.
2. Under the Operator lines → cool shots. Under the Father lines → warm shots. Start cross-cutting cool/warm early and **cut faster** as you approach "My son is neurodivergent."
3. At **"You don't manage it. You show up for it"** → stop cutting. Hold the single longest warm shot (shot 8) still. This is the collapse — the whole film has been building to this one unmoving frame.
4. Confession section → the closest warm shots (16), ending on the look-to-lens + fade to black (17).
5. **Grade:** push the Operator clips cool/blue with dark blacks; push the Father clips warm amber (`#C4621A`), soft. Add light grain over everything.
6. **No music** (optionally one quiet low note under the collapse — remove it if it feels like a trailer). Keep the ending silent.
7. Runtime target ~90s. For the website CH02, add the mono timecode/REC overlay and subtitles from `interview-transcript.txt`, and reveal the "Talk to Timothy" line on the final black-ish frame.

---

## Shoot-day running order (so you're not re-lighting constantly)
1. Record **all voiceover** first, while your voice is fresh and the room is quiet.
2. Set **cool** light → shoot Block 1 (all Operator shots + inserts).
3. Re-light **warm** → shoot Block 2 (all Father shots).
4. Stay warm, push in close → shoot Block 3 (confession) last.
5. Grab all **room tone / audio** at the end.

## Quick quality checks before you wrap
- **Sound off, can you tell which world each clip is?** (cool vs warm, wide vs close). If not, exaggerate the lighting difference.
- **No clip shows your face fully lit** except the final look-to-lens beat.
- You got **all five matched pairs** (so the cross-cut has something to cut on).
- The **four pull-quote lines** are recorded clean and land clearly.
- Watch it back cold: does it feel like it's *selling*? If yes, slow the cuts and drop any music.
